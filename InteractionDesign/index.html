AI Collaborations

AI Generated Images Are Here

Stable Diffusion, Midjouney and Dall-E are just the beginning.
Machine learning is advancing by leaps and bounds.

But the current interaction models are limiting the medium’s capacity for creative expression.

The full power of the system is hidden from the user, and people are not able to use meaningful abstractions.

The Interaction Problem is Fundamental

The complexity of the interface's input — a descriptive text string — does not match the richness of the model's output — a huge range of visual output ranging over content and style. Output does not reflect the user's intent.

Faced with this mismatch of intended image to possible output, people improvise. Their prompts get longer and more complicated. People look for secret key words and prompt hacks to bend the output towards their imagination. They randomly generate image after image in an attempt to find the lucky mix of parameters to realize the image they have in their head.

Beyond the quality of the model, language has its limits as an interface into a fundamentally visual medium.

A Medium of Dialog:

Human Intelligence in Creative Partnership with Machine Visual Intelligence

Diffusion shows us that machines now exhibit their own creative process. They don’t just record human intent for how pixels should be. They generate their own work. This calls for a new paradigm of human computer interaction.

People can now work with a computer in the same way that an art director would work with a talented illustrator. In an interaction language which is fundamentally iconic (visual) and symbolic (linguistic.)

We’re entering the era of computer as creative visual partner.

All partnerships rest on how good the communication is.

We propose the right medium is a medium of visual dialog.

What does a visual conversation between human and machine intelligence look like?

Human intelligence freely operates at different levels of abstractions. People zoom in and out of scope. They switch contexts and synthesize new combinations.

Machine Intelligence rests on complicated mathematical representations of images and text. Style, theme and content are manifested through a complex set of mathematical operations.

A meaningful conversation between these two beings needs to create a bridge to connect the disparate strengths and abilities of the two parties.

What does this look like?

  • Representing information in a mutually intelligible medium
  • Exposing operations on latent space to the humana
  • Creating operations to modulate context and scope for the machine

For things like color, size, and shape, we don’t need to know how those are encoded in latent space. We need a set of operations which allows us to express those properties.

For the countless variations on a motif we need a way to navigate through how the machine represents them.

Building a medium for Dialog

Image models are here.

Text is a great start, but it’s not sufficient as an interface.

We want image models to be creative partners.

And we need to be able to talk to our creative partners.

We propose the fundamental problem in unlocking the promise of diffusion-like models is developing a medium where the abstractions of humans can connect to the abstractions of machines.
